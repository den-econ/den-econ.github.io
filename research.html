Research

Our areas of focus, working papers, and published work

Research Areas

🌏

Development Economics

We study the determinants of long-run growth, poverty, and inequality in developing countries. Using nationally representative surveys and administrative data, we examine how policies and economic shocks affect the welfare of households across the income distribution.

🏛️

Public Finance & Fiscal Policy

Our team evaluates tax policy, government expenditure, and fiscal decentralisation. We assess how public budgets shape service delivery, local governance, and regional economic disparities in Indonesia.

🌱

Environmental & Climate Economics

We analyse the economic impacts of climate change, natural disasters, and environmental regulation. Current projects include the distributional effects of carbon pricing and the economics of green industrial policy in ASEAN.

📦

International Trade & Investment

Our trade researchers investigate how tariff changes, global value chains, and foreign direct investment affect domestic firms, workers, and household welfare in emerging economies.

💻

Digital Economy & Financial Inclusion

Rapid digital adoption offers new opportunities for financial inclusion. We study how digital payments, fintech, and e-commerce transform consumption, savings, and labour market outcomes in rural and urban Indonesia.

📈

Applied Econometrics & Methods

We develop and apply modern empirical methods — instrumental variables, regression discontinuity, difference-in-differences, synthetic control, and machine-learning approaches — to causal identification in economics.

Working Papers

Pre-publication work open for comments

Digital Financial Inclusion and Rural Consumption Smoothing: Evidence from Indonesian Villages
Krisna Gupta, Budi Santoso, Anisa Widyani
Working Paper · 2026
Using a randomised rollout of mobile banking services across 1 200 Indonesian villages, we estimate the causal effect of digital financial access on consumption volatility, precautionary savings, and risk-sharing among low-income rural households.
Digital Finance RCT Rural Development
Commodity Boom, Dutch Disease, and Structural Change in Indonesia: 2000–2022
Arya Pratama, Siti Aminah
Working Paper · 2025
This paper documents how the 2000s commodity super-cycle reshaped Indonesia's industrial structure and investigates whether resource windfalls crowded out manufacturing via real exchange-rate appreciation.
Natural Resources Structural Transformation Indonesia
Minimum Wage Hikes and Informality: A Bunching Estimator for the Indonesian Labour Market
Dinda Rachmania, Yudo Prabowo
Working Paper · 2025
We exploit kinks in the minimum wage schedule to identify labour-demand elasticities and assess the extent to which formal wage floors push workers into informal employment.
Labour Economics Informality Bunching

Published Research

Peer-reviewed articles, book chapters, and policy reports

Trade Liberalisation and Household Welfare in Indonesia: Evidence from District-Level Panel Data
Krisna Gupta, Dinda Rachmania, Arya Pratama
Journal of Development Economics · 2024
We exploit variation in tariff reductions to identify the causal effect of trade openness on household consumption and poverty rates across 500+ Indonesian districts from 2000–2020.
Trade Indonesia Causal Inference
Fiscal Decentralisation, Local Governance Quality, and Public Service Delivery
Siti Aminah, Budi Santoso, Krisna Gupta
World Development · 2024
Using a regression-discontinuity design around population thresholds that determine local government tiers, we estimate the effect of decentralisation on health and education outcomes.
Fiscal Policy Public Finance RDD
Carbon Pricing in a Developing Economy: Labour Market and Distributional Effects
Dinda Rachmania, Yudo Prabowo
Energy Policy · 2023
A CGE model calibrated to the Indonesian input-output table assesses how alternative carbon-pricing schemes affect wages, employment, and income distribution across sectors.
Climate CGE Labour Economics
The Returns to Education and Skills in Indonesia: A Meta-Analysis
Budi Santoso, Anisa Widyani
Asian Development Review · 2023
Combining 80 estimates from 30 studies, we document heterogeneous returns to schooling across gender, region, and sector of employment, and assess publication-bias using funnel-plot regression.
Education Human Capital Meta-analysis
Non-Tariff Barriers, Global Value Chains, and Firm-Level Productivity in ASEAN
Arya Pratama, Krisna Gupta
Review of International Economics · 2022
Trade GVC Firm Productivity

Data & Replication

We are committed to open and reproducible research

💾

Datasets

Cleaned and harmonised datasets used in our research are deposited in public repositories for replication and secondary use.

🖥️

Code

Replication code for our papers is available on GitHub. We use R, Python, and Stata.

📋

Pre-registration

We pre-register our studies on the AEA RCT Registry and OSF to ensure transparency and reduce publication bias.
