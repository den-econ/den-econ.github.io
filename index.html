What We Do

Rigorous research. Actionable insights.

🌏

Development Economics

Studying the drivers of growth, poverty reduction, and inequality in emerging economies with a focus on South-East Asia.

📈

Applied Econometrics

Developing and applying cutting-edge empirical methods — causal inference, machine learning, and big-data approaches.

🏛️

Policy Analysis

Evaluating fiscal, trade, and social policies to provide evidence-based recommendations to governments and institutions.

🤝

Capacity Building

Training the next generation of economists through workshops, seminars, and collaborative research programmes.

Recent Publications

Selected recent work from our team

Trade Liberalisation and Household Welfare in Indonesia: Evidence from District-Level Panel Data
Krisna Gupta, Dinda Rachmania, Arya Pratama
Journal of Development Economics · 2024
We exploit variation in tariff reductions to identify the causal effect of trade openness on household consumption and poverty rates across 500+ Indonesian districts from 2000–2020.
Trade Indonesia Causal Inference
Fiscal Decentralisation, Local Governance Quality, and Public Service Delivery
Siti Aminah, Budi Santoso, Krisna Gupta
World Development · 2024
Using a regression-discontinuity design around population thresholds that determine local government tiers, we estimate the effect of decentralisation on health and education outcomes.
Fiscal Policy Public Finance RDD
Carbon Pricing in a Developing Economy: Labour Market and Distributional Effects
Dinda Rachmania, Yudo Prabowo
Energy Policy · 2023
A CGE model calibrated to the Indonesian input-output table assesses how alternative carbon-pricing schemes affect wages, employment, and income distribution across sectors.
Climate CGE Labour Economics
