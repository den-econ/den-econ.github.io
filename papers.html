Research

Working papers, publications, and ongoing projects

Working Papers

  • Earnings Responses to Payroll Tax Changes: Evidence from Administrative Records
    Alex Rivera, Lena Müller — NBER Working Paper 12345, 2025
    Labor supply Taxation Bunching
    We use administrative earnings records linked to payroll tax filings to estimate behavioral responses to kink points in the payroll tax schedule. Using a bunching estimator, we find a compensated elasticity of taxable earnings of 0.18 at the primary kink, driven predominantly by self-employed workers. Extensive-margin responses are concentrated among low-wage part-time workers.
    PDF SSRN NBER
  • School Quality and Long-Run Labor Market Outcomes: Quasi-Experimental Evidence
    Sara Chen, Carlos Mendez — Working Paper, 2025
    Education Human capital Quasi-experiment
    We exploit discontinuities in school-assignment rules to estimate the causal effect of attending a higher-quality public school on adult earnings, employment, and educational attainment. Using administrative linked employer–employee data, we find that attending a one-standard-deviation higher-quality school increases annual earnings at age 30 by approximately 7%, with larger effects for students from lower-income households.
    PDF SSRN
  • Monopsony Power and Minimum Wages: Evidence from Linked Employer–Employee Data
    Marcus Webb — Working Paper, 2026
    Monopsony Minimum wage Labor markets
    This paper develops and estimates a model of monopsonistic competition in local labor markets to evaluate the employment effects of minimum wage increases. Estimating the model using matched employer–employee data from 2000–2022, I find that moderate minimum wage increases reduce employment by less than neoclassical models predict, consistent with the presence of employer market power.

Publications

  • Job Displacement and Long-Run Earnings Losses: Heterogeneity by Firm Quality
    Alex Rivera, Elena Vasquez — American Economic Review, 114(3): 812–849, 2024
    Job displacement Firm quality Earnings dynamics
    We document that earnings losses following job displacement are highly heterogeneous and depend on the quality of the pre-displacement employer. Workers displaced from high-productivity, high-wage firms suffer larger and more persistent losses than those from low-productivity firms, consistent with a model in which firm-specific rents account for a substantial share of compensation.
    Journal PDF (pre-print) Replication package
  • Microfinance and Female Entrepreneurship: Experimental Evidence from Ghana
    Sara Chen, James Osei — Journal of Development Economics, 168: 103005, 2024
    Microfinance Gender RCT Africa
    We report results from a randomized evaluation of a group-lending microfinance program targeting women entrepreneurs in rural Ghana. After two years, treated households show significant increases in business investment, profits, and women's decision-making autonomy, with no evidence of adverse effects on household consumption.
    Journal PDF (pre-print) AEA RCT Registry
  • Wage Dispersion and Search Frictions: A Structural Approach
    Marcus Webb — Review of Economic Studies, 91(2): 1102–1138, 2024
    Search & matching Wage dispersion Structural estimation
    I structurally estimate an equilibrium on-the-job search model using matched employer–employee panel data to decompose wage dispersion into worker heterogeneity, firm heterogeneity, and search frictions. Frictions account for roughly 30% of residual wage variance, with this share falling over the sample period as online job-search platforms reduced search costs.
    Journal PDF (pre-print)
